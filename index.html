Democracy and Enviromental Consciousness
in the United States.

Every four years, our country divides in two, and sometimes more, by choosing who to elect as our next president. Though it’s always a huge debate, a topic recommended to avoid at family dinners, this past election seemed even more divisive and extreme than usual. More topics became bigger focuses of this election in particular, including the environment. We as a planet have reached a point where it is too late to reverse the environmental damage we’ve created in order to avoid extreme consequences. Instead, we must take every measure to mitigate our impact and lessen the impending damage.

It is a common opinion, though not necessarily based on extensive research, that democrats are more likely to be environmentally conscious. Democratic politicians are more known for their environmental protection laws and initiatives, and eco-friendly people are also often liberals as well. These "facts" vary though, and there are always exceptions.

Can we find a true correlation between voting tendencies and environmental consciousness?

This project aims to take a closer look at voter turnout and voting results in the 2016 Presidential Election, and see if there is any correlation with how environmentally friendly or conscious states are.
